PARAFLOW

Showcase of Our Virtual Reality Paragliding Simulator

As Project Manager for the Research Laboratory in Arts and Science (LARAS), I successfully secured fundings from the Brussels Region to participate to the I Love Science Festival, an event designed to promote sciences towards youngsters, and present ParaFlow, our virtual reality paragliding simulator!

I was responsible for the the communication as well as the financial and administrative management and reporting of the project. The aim of the project was to have fun while learning some useful concepts : together with the presentation of our paragliding demonstrator, we developed some educational materials linked to the flying experience, the technologies we used to create it (virtual reality programming with C# and Unity, 3D printing for controllers, fan to simulate wind) and organised 10 minutes Q&A sessions prior to flying the simulator.

As part of this materials, I also produced an original animation using Unity to explain how to fly with the simulator.

To maximise outreach, on top of our digital campaign, we also managed to get featured in a Belgian popular kid TV show.



In total, more than 650 young (and less young) visitors took a ride with us!

  • Date: November 2018-April 2019
  • Position : LARAS Project Manager
  • Budget: 30.000 euros

ROLLER COASTER Z

In-house Roller Coaster Prototype

As a Developer at LARAS, I was tasked with developing a demonstrator using a Motion Platform designed by Motion Systems and came up with Roller Coaster Z, a virtual reality roller coaster simulation taking place on Mars!

Working with C# and Unity, I created an original ride, augmented with a DMX-based fan to create an immersive and fun experience for our students and visitors.

Roller Coaster Z was featured at the XR MeetUp (@IRISIB on May 2019), the gathering of European VR professionals, and was met with great enthousiasm!

  • Date: May 2019
  • Position : Developer
  • Technologies : C# and Unity

EU-level Political Campaigns

Convey Workers' Position on the Future of EU Maritime Transport Policy

As acting Policy Officer responsible for Maritime Transport at the European Transport Workers' Federation, I carried out a 3-steps political campaign aimed at ensuring that workers' interests would be taken into account when shaping the future of EU Maritime Transport :

  • Step 1: ETF Response to the Public Consultation on the Mid-Term Review of the EU Maritime Transport Strategy - Position paper answering the questions of the European Commission as how our particular members (seafarers from all over Europe) viewed the future of EU maritime transport, and what priorities should the Commission embrace to ensure quality shipping.
  • Step 2: ETF Manifesto on a Social Agenda for Quality Shipping in Europe - Shorter position paper setting out the priorities of the ETF Maritime Transport Section in the future, aimed at other interests groups and the press.
  • Step 3: Joint Workers-Employers Position on the Mid-Term Review of the EU Maritime Transport Strategy - Position paper secured after lenghty negociations with the employers' representative organisation (ECSA). Paper adopted in the context of the EU Social Dialogue, and in which we outline our common views on what the future of maritime transport should look like.
  • Date: 2015
  • Position: Policy Officer
  • Targets: EU Institutions, Interests Groups, Press

EU Project Management

Raise Awareness and Educate about Harassment and Bullying in the Shipping Sector

As a Policy Assistant at the ETF, I managed a project carried out jointly by social partners (ourselves and ECSA, the shipowners' association) aiming at producing a training toolkit to fight against harassment and bullying amongst seafarers, one of the top priorities set out in the context of our EU Social Dialogue Committee.
The training toolkit is made of:

  • a Training Video subtitled in 11 languages, and an associated Workbook for trainers, available in 23 languages




  • the Guidelines to Shipping Companies regarding the best ways to identify incidents of harassment and bullying and deal with them, also available in 23 languages

We then went all over Europe (London, Tallinn, Helsinki, Rijeka and Marseille) to present the training toolkit to seafarers, disseminate its results and gather more inputs and feedbacks. A final event gathering more than 200 stakeholders was organised, in attendance of European Commission officials and Members of the European Parliament, the press and many seafarers who came to share their experiences. Well-known maritime news outlets such as Safety4Sea reported on the event, putting the issue high on the agenda of the shipping community.

  • Date: January 2014
  • Position : Policy Assistant
  • Budget: 30.000 euros
