PARAFLOW

Showcase of Our Virtual Reality Paragliding Simulator

As a Project Manager for the Research Laboratory in Arts and Science (LARAS), I successfully secured fundings from the Brussels Region to participate to the I Love Science Festival, an event designed to promote sciences towards youngsters.

Together with the presentation of our virtual reality paragliding demonstrator, we developed some educational materials linked to the flying experience and how we came to create it (virtual reality, 3D printing), and I created an original animation using Unity to explain how to fly with the simulator.

To maximise outreach, we also managed to get featured in a Belgian popular kid TV show.



In total, more than 650 young (and less young) visitors took a ride with us!

  • Date: November 2018-April 2019
  • Position : LARAS Project Manager
  • Budget: 30.000 euros

EU-level Political Campaign

Convey Workers' Position on the Future of EU Maritime Transport

As acting Policy Officer responsible for Maritime Transport at the European Transport Workers' Federation, I carried out a 3-steps political campaign aimed at ensuring that workers' interests would be taken into account when shaping the future of EU Maritime Transport :

  • Step 1: ETF Response to the Public Consultation on the Mid-Term Review of the EU Maritime Transport Strategy - Position paper answering the questions of the European Commission as how our particular members (seafarers from all over Europe) viewed the future of EU maritime transport, and what priorities should the Commission embrace to ensure quality shipping.
  • Step 2: ETF Manifesto on a Social Agenda for Quality Shipping in Europe - Shorter position paper setting out the priorities of the ETF Maritime Transport Section in the future, aimed at other interests groups and the press.
  • Step 3: Joint Workers-Employers Position on the Mid-Term Review of the EU Maritime Transport Strategy - Position paper secured after lenghty negociations with the employers' representative organisation (ECSA). Paper adopted in the context of the EU Social Dialogue, and in which we outline our common views on what the future of maritime transport should look like.
  • Date: 2015
  • Position: Policy Officer
  • Targets: EU Institutions, Interests Groups, Press

Training Toolkit Against Bullying

Raise Awareness and Educate about Harassment and Bullying in the Shipping Sector

As a Policy Assistant at the ETF, I managed a project carried out jointly by social partners (ourselves and ECSA, the shipowners' association) aiming at producing a training toolkit to fight against harassment and bullying amongst seafarers, one of the top priorities set out in the context of our EU Social Dialogue Committee.
The training toolkit is made of:

  • a Training Video subtitled in 11 languages, and an associated Workbook for trainers, available in 23 languages




  • the an Guidelines to Shipping Companies regarding the best ways to identify incidents of harassment and bullying and deal with them, also available in 23 languages

We then went all over Europe (London, Tallinn, Helsinki, Rijeka and Marseille) to present the training toolkit to seafarers, disseminated its results and gather more inputs and feedbacks. A final event gathering more than 200 stakeholders was organised, in attendance of European Commission officials and Members of the European Parliament, the press and many seafarers who came to share their experiences. Well-known maritime news outlets such as Safety4Sea reported on the event, putting the issue high on the agenda of the shipping community.

  • Date: January 2014
  • Position : Policy Assistant
  • Budget: 30.000 euros
