  • 1984-2008

    Academic Record

    I have also always took an interest in politics : I still remember this class assignment we had when we were 8 years old, writing to the then French President M. Mitterand, and being the only one getting an official reply from his office. How happy was little me!
    So, after getting my Economic and Social Sciences baccalauréat, I headed to a special program at the Aix-en-Provence Law University, where I specialised in International and European Law. The European construction was an innovative political object that I saw as the next step in building a possibly federal Europe. That were my ideals, and so I eagerly spent a year in Rotterdam (2006-2007) thanks to the Erasmus Exchange Program : the incredible people I met from all over Europe (and the world), the courses and late nights discussions in English, the feeling that we were building the union everyday and that our lifes were full of opportunities. It is one of the most exciting, revealing, fun and interesting experiences I have had in my life. Convinced that EU Affairs was my calling, I successfully achieved a Master 2 in European Law Engineering, took my luggages and headed for Brussels, the Capitale of Europe.

    More

  • 2009-2011

    The Brussels EU Bubble

    As a typical newcomer, I started out as an intern, at the Representation Office of three French Regions, where I discovered the basics of lobbying : legislative and news monitoring, writing of briefings, meetings with other regional offices and Commission's policy officers and Parliamentary assistants. I also discovered the EU-bubble social life, with the legendary "Place Lux", and it felt like Erasmus all over again : surrounded by expats from all Europre, eager to make our place in this exciting environment. After a brief gig as a translator at EurActiv, an online media specialized on EU Affairs, I worked for the European Geotherman Energy Council, where I could deepen my lobbying skills as well as my knowledge of environmental issues.

    More

  • 2011-2015

    Building Up My Skills

    My core experience as a lobbyist comes from working for 5 years as an Assistant at the European Transport Workers' Federation, which gathers transport workers' unions from all over Europe. I had the chance to represent more specifically seafarers, dockers and fishermen, at a time where the future of the EU legislative frameworks governing these sectors were about to be revised. Managing to get an amendment in a EU regulation to improve fishermen working conditions, leading a project to fight bullying and harassment onboard ships or lobby to make sure that human capital was at the center of the EU Maritime transport strategy were some of my most thrilling experiences as a lobbyist. I was especially fond of working in the context of Social Dialogue Committees, with workers' and employers' representatives discussing and acting on strategic issues for their sectors : it could be hard negotiations sometimes, and it helped me learn the "game of negotiation".

    More

  • 2016-2017

    Transition to Digital

    As years passed, the importance of digital communication and social media in our work, especially as project manager, started to grow to eventually overcome the more traditional communication tools. I started to really look into those tools and read some litterature about the digital revolution, as the digital behemoth became the biggest companies the world had ever seen. One particular book about AI caught my attention, and out of intellectual curiosity, I decided to follow a training on the Basics of Coding, willing to understand what was happening behind the screen. It was a revelation for me, a whole new world of creative freedom. After an internship and a 4-months long trainign course in .NET, during which I secured a Microsoft Certification, I joined the Research Laboratory for Arts and Sciences.

PARAFLOW

Showcase of Our Virtual Reality Paragliding Simulator

As a Project Manager for the Research Laboratory in Arts and Science (LARAS), I successfully secured fundings from the Brussels Region to participate to the I Love Science Festival, an event designed to promote sciences towards youngsters.

Together with the presentation of our virtual reality paragliding demonstrator, we developed some educational materials linked to the flying experience and how we came to create it (virtual reality, 3D printing), and I created an original animation using Unity to explain how to fly with the simulator.

To maximise outreach, we also managed to get featured in a Belgian popular kid TV show.



In total, more than 650 young (and less young) visitors took a ride with us!

  • Date: November 2018-April 2019
  • Position : LARAS Project Manager
  • Budget: 30.000 euros

EU-level Political Campaign

Convey Workers' Position on the Future of EU Maritime Transport

As acting Policy Officer responsible for Maritime Transport at the European Transport Workers' Federation, I carried out a 3-steps political campaign aimed at ensuring that workers' interests would be taken into account when shaping the future of EU Maritime Transport :

  • Step 1: ETF Response to the Public Consultation on the Mid-Term Review of the EU Maritime Transport Strategy - Position paper answering the questions of the European Commission as how our particular members (seafarers from all over Europe) viewed the future of EU maritime transport, and what priorities should the Commission embrace to ensure quality shipping.
  • Step 2: ETF Manifesto on a Social Agenda for Quality Shipping in Europe - Shorter position paper setting out the priorities of the ETF Maritime Transport Section in the future, aimed at other interests groups and the press.
  • Step 3: Joint Workers-Employers Position on the Mid-Term Review of the EU Maritime Transport Strategy - Position paper secured after lenghty negociations with the employers' representative organisation (ECSA). Paper adopted in the context of the EU Social Dialogue, and in which we outline our common views on what the future of maritime transport should look like.
  • Date: 2015
  • Position: Policy Officer
  • Targets: EU Institutions, Interests Groups, Press

Training Toolkit Against Bullying

Raise Awareness and Educate about Harassment and Bullying in the Shipping Sector

As a Policy Assistant at the ETF, I managed a project carried out jointly by social partners (ourselves and ECSA, the shipowners' association) aiming at producing a training toolkit to fight against harassment and bullying amongst seafarers, one of the top priorities set out in the context of our EU Social Dialogue Committee.
The training toolkit is made of:

  • a Training Video subtitled in 11 languages, and an associated Workbook for trainers, available in 23 languages




  • the an Guidelines to Shipping Companies regarding the best ways to identify incidents of harassment and bullying and deal with them, also available in 23 languages

We then went all over Europe (London, Tallinn, Helsinki, Rijeka and Marseille) to present the training toolkit to seafarers, disseminated its results and gather more inputs and feedbacks. A final event gathering more than 200 stakeholders was organised, in attendance of European Commission officials and Members of the European Parliament, the press and many seafarers who came to share their experiences. Well-known maritime news outlets such as Safety4Sea reported on the event, putting the issue high on the agenda of the shipping community.

  • Date: January 2014
  • Position : Policy Assistant
  • Budget: 30.000 euros
